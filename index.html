As a history major, there have been numerous times where the professor assigns students complex primary sources for interpretation. These primary sources range from all periods of human history, from the first manuscripts written at Mesopotamia to the contemporary article of modernity. These primary sources provide all forms of perspectives. They give readers an insight to why certain events occured and how those at the time were foolish to prevent a catastrophe from happening. Other documents provide readers information how certain civilizations united in order to overcome challenges. Overall, being a history student provides one with an opportunity to sharpen one's reading, writitng, and analytical skills since it is demanding.
